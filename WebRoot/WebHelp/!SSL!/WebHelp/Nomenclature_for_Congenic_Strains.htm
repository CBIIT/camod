Nomenclature for Congenic Strains

Congenic strains are designated by a compound symbol consisting of the host strain

designation separated by a period from the donor strain and followed by a hyphen

and the symbol of the differential locus or loci and allele(s) in italics, e.g.,

B10.129P-H12 b . Abbreviated strain symbols are most often used to shorten the strain

name. In cases where the donor strain is not an inbred or is complex, the symbol

Cg, denoting congenic, is used. Strains assume congenic nomenclature after five generations

of backcrossing and are considered incipient congenics until the tenth generation

is reached. The generation number is included in the strain detail to help distinguish

incipient congenic strains from full congenic strains. The Jackson Laboratory supplies

a large number of histocompatibility congenic strains. In some cases, multiple lines

were derived from the same donor strain and are distinguished by a number and/or

letter in parentheses; e.g., B10.129P-H11 b (10M)/Sn and B10.129P-H46 b H47 b (21M)/Sn.
