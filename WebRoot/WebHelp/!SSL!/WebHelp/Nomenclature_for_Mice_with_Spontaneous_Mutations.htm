Nomenclature for Mice with Spontaneous Mutations

Gene symbols are an abbreviation of the gene name and are set in italics. Symbols

for recessive genes always begin with a lower case letter. All other symbols (for

dominant, semi-dominant, or co-dominant genes) begin with an uppercase letter followed

by lower case letters. For mutant genes, the gene and first mutant allele symbol

are the same until the gene is cloned (see Nomenclature for Cloned Mutations below).

Different alleles of a gene may be represented by a superscript to the gene symbol.

Strain names for mice carrying spontaneous mutations are designated in one of the

following three ways:

Example 1: C57BL/6J-A w-J

Description: When a mutation is maintained on an inbred strain background by mating

homozygotes together, the gene symbol is given once. All offspring from this mating

are affected mutant mice (phenotypically manifest the genetic mutation).

Example 2: C57BL/6J-Ghrhrlit /+

Description: When a mutation is maintained on an inbred strain background by brother

x sister matings with forced heterozygosis, the gene symbol is followed by the designation:

/+. In the case of a recessive mutation, 25% of the offspring are affected if the

strain is maintained by intercrossing heterozygotes and 50% are affected if it is

maintained by mating heterozygotes to homozygotes. 50% of the offspring are affected

if the mutant gene is transmitted dominantly or semidominantly.

Example 3: B6.Cg-ax J or B6C3Fe-a/a-Csf1 op

Description: When the strain is maintained by continued backcrossing to an inbred

strain or F1 hybrid, the symbol(s) for the introduced gene(s) is given once. Progeny

from strains maintained by backcrossing to F1 hybrids are not true F1's and are segregating

at all genes for which the F1 parental strains differ.
