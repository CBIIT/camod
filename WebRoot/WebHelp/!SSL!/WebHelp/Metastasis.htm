Metastasis

To enter or edit  the Metastasis information, a subheading under the Histopathology category, for a animal model:

 

  1. Click Histopathology from the list of Submit and Edit Models menu options on the left side of the caMOD viewing window. These menu options display only after you have clicked the (model) Submit button in other caMOD viewers.

 

Note:

  • Subheadings indicate organ metastasis; if listed as a subheading, the metastasis originated in the organ in the top-level heading.

  • Blue text = an item that has already been entered into the caMOD database.

  • Brown text = places where you can enter metastases.

 

 

Example:  This model has a tumor in the mammary gland, already entered in the database. It also has a tumor in the liver. The liver cancer has metastasized to the heart, data which has also been entered into the database. To add associated metastases, click on the brown text in either location.

 

  1. Click Enter Associated Metastasis (brown text) under the appropriate organ indicated in blue text. In the Metastasis form, enter or select the appropriate information about your model in the text fields. Items with an asterisk are required fields.

 

 

Metastasis Fields

Description

*Site of Lesion/Tumor

Click the Select button. Select from the disease vocabulary tree that opens the site of the lesion or tumor used as the source for this metastasis specimen.

*Diagnosis

Click the Select button. Select from the disease vocabulary tree that opens the diagnosis  assigned to this metastasis specimen.

Age of Metastasis Onset

Age of the animal when the metastasis is first detectable. Enter age information in the text box or from the drop-down list.

 

Note: If the time unit you are looking for is not available in the drop down list, manually enter the time unit together with the number in the text field. When you return to the page, your time unit entry that you selected or entered manually will be displayed in the text input field. Should you decide to update the unit information, you must delete the current time unit entry from the text field before selecting or entering the new information. Should you fail to delete first, the new time unit entry will be added to the old entry.

Average Weight of Metastasis (mg)

  

Enter the average weight of the metastasis.

Average Volume of Metastasis (mm3)

Enter the average volume of the metastasis.

Metastasis Incidence Over Lifetime (%)

Percentage of the population that develops metastases. (Numbers only)

Survival Information

Life expectancy for the animals with metastasized tumors.

Gross Description/Macroscopic Description

Describe structural and functional changes resulting from the disease processes; can be detected by the unaided eye.

Microscopic Description

Describe structural and functional changes resulting from the disease processes; could not be detected by the unaided eye.

Genetic Alterations found in Tumor

Describe genetic alterations found in the metastasis other then genetically engineered

changes.

Method of Observation

List the method(s) by which the metastasis was discovered. Note: This field becomes writable only after you have entered information in the Genetic Alterations found in Tumor field.

Comparative Data from Other Species

Describe occurrences found in humans or other species.

Comment

Comment on the contents of this page or use this field to add data for this model. You can also enter information that you would like to share but that did not fit in any other field.

 

  1. Click the Submit button to execute the submission of the model information.

 

  1. Click Reset to return the form to default settings: If you are entering data for the first time, Reset clears the form (resets it to its defaults). If you are changing data for an existing model, Reset returns the form back to the unmodified state (the default).
