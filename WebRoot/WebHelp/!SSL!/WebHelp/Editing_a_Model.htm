Editing a Model

An Editor is a registered user, usually scientifically trained, who evaluates the model entry for scientific correctness. An Editor is appointed by the Coordinator.

 

Responsibilities of an Editor:

 

  • When notified that a model is ready for editing/reviewing, the Editor examines the model to see if it satisfies criteria to be made available to the public.

  • The Editor considers any comments made by the Screener, which are passed along in the email from the Coordinator.

  • The Editor may determine that the model cannot be made acceptable for publication in the database. For example, the model may be a prank.

  • If the model has correctable defects, it may be necessary to communicate by email with the submitter to make the model acceptable.

Correspondence and telephone contact with the submitters may be conducted through the Coordinator.

  • Editors are responsible for maintaining a paper trail or log of email exchanges regarding the models they review.

  • The Editor may elect to review a model without assistance, or he or she can request assistance from other subject matter experts.

  • Only the Editor has the authority to release the model to the intended audience.

 

Work flow:

  1. You, as the Editor, receive an email with the information that a model has been assigned to her/him for editing.

  2. Open caMOD and log in.

  3. Select Admin from the top of the viewer.

  4. On the Admin page, select Database administration; enter user name and password.

A new page opens, displaying links to the assigned models.

  1. Open the model detail pages and review all detail pages. An underlined format indicates the categories that have information submitted with the model.

    * General Information

    * Genetic Description

    * Carcinogenic Agents

    * Publication

    * Histopathology

    * Therapeutic Approaches

    * Cell Lines

    * Images

Links to the detail pages are provided on top of each page. The current page is marked by the purple background of the link.

  1. Make your decision after you have reviewed the model.

The Editor can

    * approve a model--By approving the model the editor releases the model to the intended audience.

    * request more information about the model--The editor may determine that the model cannot be made acceptable for publication in the database.  If the model has correctable defects, it may be necessary to communicate by email with the submitter to make the model acceptable. Correspondence and telephone contact with the submitters may be conducted through the coordinator.

7. Go to the Administration Roles page and indicate your evaluation of the model.
