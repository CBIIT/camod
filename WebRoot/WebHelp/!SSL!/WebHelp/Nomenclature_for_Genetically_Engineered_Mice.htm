Nomenclature for Genetically Engineered Mice

Transgenes formerly began with the genetic background followed by the mode of incorporation

(TgN for nonhomologous insertion, TgR for insertion via infection with a retroviral

vector, and TgH for homologous recombination), followed by designation for the DNA

insert (most often the gene symbol with or without an indication of the promoter),

a number indicating the founder line, and a laboratory registration code. For example,

the transgenic strain C57BL/6-TgN(APOA1)1Rub is on a C57BL/6 genetic background with

the human APOA1 gene (human gene symbols are designated by italicized capital letters)

incorporated into the mouse genome by nonhomologous insertion (pronuclear injection

of DNA). The founder number is "1" and the mutation was made by Dr. E.M. Rubin (lab

registration code Rub).

In January, 2001, the International Committee on Standardized Genetic Nomenclature

for Mice decided that transgenic alleles will be in the following format:

o Tg denoting transgene

o In parentheses, the official gene symbol of the inserted DNA

o The laboratory's line or founder designation or a serial number

o The Lab Code of the originating lab

For example, Tg(Zfp38)D1Htz is a transgene containing the mouse Zfp38 gene, in line

D1 reported by Nathaniel Heintz.

In this catalog we have not changed existing names but have used the new nomenclature

rules for all new strains.

Chemically induced and targeted mutations follow the same guidelines as described

for mice with spontaneous mutations (see next paragraph). Alleles are designated

by the chemical mutagen or by the abbreviation "tm" (targeted mutation) followed

by a number and the laboratory registration code and are superscripted to the gene

symbol (e.g., Apoa1 tm1Unc ). Please note, for space reasons the allele designation

(tm# lab code) has been deleted from the SYMBOL column in this catalog.
